# Quora 回答 3：Why do some religions walk clockwise around Mount Kailash while others walk counter-clockwise?

---

**Short answer:** Because Mount Kailash is claimed by multiple religions with independent traditions. The directional difference reflects a historical negotiation of sacred space, not a random choice.

**The four directions:**

- **Hindus and Tibetan Buddhists**: Clockwise. For Hindus, this follows solar movement and the right-hand path (dakshinachara). For Buddhists, it aligns with the direction of prayer wheels and merit accumulation.
- **Bön practitioners**: Counter-clockwise. This is the critical detail—Bön claims to be Tibet's pre-Buddhist indigenous tradition, and the opposite direction may originally have marked religious independence.

**However, the origin of counter-clockwise circumambulation is debated among scholars.**

Some Tibetologists argue it was constructed *after* Buddhism arrived, as a deliberate differentiation marker. Others (particularly Bön practitioners) insist it is an ancient, original practice predating Buddhist influence. Archaeological evidence hasn't definitively settled this.

**What is certain:** The coexistence of both directions at Kailash is historically significant. The 11th-century legend of Milarepa (Buddhist) and Naro Bönchung (Bön) "contesting" the mountain—in Buddhist sources—ends with Buddhism "winning" but Bön retaining the mountain's counter-clockwise practice. Bön sources preserve a different version: coexistence, not conquest.

**For pilgrims today:** The direction you walk signals which tradition you follow. At Kailash, you will see both. No conflict, just parallel sacred practice.
