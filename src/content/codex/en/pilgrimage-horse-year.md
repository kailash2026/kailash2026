## Special Significance

In Tibetan Buddhist tradition, making a **kora (circumambulation)** around Mount Kailash during the **Tibetan Horse Year** generates merit equivalent to **thirteen circuits** in ordinary years. This tradition stems from the belief that Kailash is the mandala of Cakrasaṃvara.

## Origin of the Tradition

The **Horse Year** is considered Kailash's "birth year" as a Buddhist sacred site. It is said that Cakrasaṃvara descends upon Kailash during the Horse Year, making the mountain's spiritual power exceptionally strong.

- Mass bathing rituals at Lake Manasarovar
- Special prayer ceremonies at monasteries
- Group circumambulations and chanting sessions

## The Number Thirteen

The tradition of thirteen circuits holds special meaning:
- Thirteen is an auspicious number in Tibetan Buddhism
- It represents completeness and transcendence
- Some traditions hold that thirteen circuits purify thirteen root delusions
