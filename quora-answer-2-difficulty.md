# Quora 回答 2：How difficult is the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra physically?

---

**Short answer:** It's not technical climbing, but the altitude makes it significantly harder than the distance (52 km) suggests. Most people underestimate it until they are gasping for air at Dolma La (5,630m).

**The real difficulty is threefold:**

1. **Altitude, not distance** — The entire route is above 4,500m. AMS (Acute Mountain Sickness) is common, and Diamox doesn't work for everyone.
2. **Weather volatility** — Snow in June, sudden storms at the pass, sub-zero nights even in summer.
3. **Logistics complexity** — Permits, restricted areas, medical fitness certificates. The Indian government's official Yatra is by lottery; private routes through Nepal add cost and uncertainty.

**The "2-day" Kora is misleading.** Physically fit trekkers from sea level often struggle more than slower walkers who have acclimatized properly. The body reacts to altitude unpredictably—I've seen marathon runners humbled by the pass, and 60-year-old pilgrims finish steadily.

**Practical preparation:**
- Arrive in Tibet/Nepal border areas 3-5 days early to acclimatize
- Don't skip the medical check (mandatory for the Indian route)
- Bring a good sleeping bag rated to -15°C minimum
- Water purification tablets—the streams are glacier-fed but not sterile
